Why Recall Matters in Dropout Prediction
In dropout prediction, a False Negative — missing an at-risk student — is far more costly than a False Positive. If we classify a high-risk student as safe, they receive no intervention and may drop out. Our Random Forest model prioritises high recall to minimise missed at-risk students. The confusion matrix above shows the breakdown of correct and incorrect predictions on the held-out test set (20% of 1,200 students). XGBoost achieves similar accuracy but Random Forest provides superior interpretability via feature importance — critical for faculty to trust and act on recommendations.
